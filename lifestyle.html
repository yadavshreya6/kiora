1947

The First Stone

The story of Kaiora really starts in 1947, in a small stone house on the outskirts of Shimla. Lieutenant-Colonel Heer Malhotra Windsor, newly widowed, inherited a family trade that for generations had supplied quality textiles to hill-station officers.

Among her late husband's keepsakes were polished brass compass cases, uniform buttons, and smooth river stones he'd collected during his service. One evening, Heer took a simple quartz pebble from his belongings and carved it into a small pendant. She fastened it to a strip of leather from her family's stores, added a scrap of hand-loomed silk, and that modest piece became the first Kaiora creation.

1970s

Market Refinements

Her daughter, Devika Windsor, grew up watching that pendant take shape. In the 1970s, Devika set up a stall at the Shimla market and refined the process—cutting and polishing stones more smoothly, tightening her leatherwork, and sewing neat, reliable stitching into every strap.

She introduced small wristwatches with carved-stone bezels and simple ring-watches that blended local craft with everyday use. Word spread quietly: Kaiora pieces were never flashy, but always well made.

2025

Global Debut

By 2025, Heer's great-grandson Raj Windsor saw an opportunity to share this family craft with the world. He gathered Devika's original stone-carved designs and heirloom silks, then traveled to Mumbai, New York, and Tokyo.

He launched Kaiora as India's first truly integrated luxury house—the only homegrown brand to offer precision wristwatches, signature perfumes, and ring-watches all under one roof, each featuring the same carved-stone detail Heer first experimented with.

Today, Kaiora remains a three-generation story of practical innovation: Heer's first pendant by lamplight, Devika's market-stall refinements, and Raj's global debut. Every watch, perfume, and ring-watch carries the quiet confidence of Shimla stone and Windsor craftsmanship—an Indian legacy you can wear, time and again.
